Pillars of Creation

These are images of the famous nebula known as The Pillars of Creation, taken by both the James Webb and the Hubble telescopes. As you can see, the difference in detail is astounding. The infrared views from the JWST are able to penetrate the thick clouds of gas and pickout the thousands of stars glowing within.

Image by JWST
Image by Hubble

The Phantom Galaxy

These are images of the well known galaxy Messier 74, also known as The Phantom Galaxy. On the right is an original image by the Hubble telescope in the optical wavelengths, essentially what you would see with your own eyes. The JWST on the other hand took its image in longer wavelengths like infrared to radio waves, and "translated" them into colors we can see.

Image by JWST
Image by Hubble

The Southern Ring

NGC 3132, also known as the Southern Ring Nebula, is a massive cloud of gas that was dispersed by a dieing star. The right image was taken by the hubble in the year 1998, while the James Web's image, with its much greater clarity, was taken just this last year.
